Steampunk Class-based Game Concept


          My current style isn't very typical of video games. I love fantasy and sci-fi but I'm also pretty bored by the straight-ahead recycling of those tropes. I'm still interested in alternative styles of genre, and magical realism/surrealism, and I'll always be a staunch supporter of genre against snobs but I don't really draw many wizards or cowboys these days, so sometimes my application to video game companies can be a little hard. I always get told "these would look great for one of those indie games but we don't make those." So I included these just in case, to demonstrate that I do in fact know how to draw a steampunk wizard guy.

          I played a lot of Team Fortress 2 and loved the class system, so I made a steampunk version of my own. 3 base classes (melee, range, and support) with 3 sub-specialities each. This was back when I used to ink everything comic style and was still heavily inspired by anime. I think one of the biggest changes in my style has been a greater focus on higher-level design concerns for illustration. For instance, unlike TF2, these characters lack distinct silhouettes. They can't be easily identified from a distance. There's also a lot of visual clutter, which makes parsing what is important and unimportant harder.

          I've also included a more recent concept piece from a project I've been working on and off with a friend. It's also class based, and informed by genre, but I wanted to have a more diverse set of inspirations and ideas in it. Though this style of concepting is simpler, it's much faster and I think produces more creative ideas which focus on the most essential parts of forming the visual design of a piece.
